Overview

What Is a Renovation Loan?

A renovation loan (also called a fixer-upper loan) lets you combine financing for the home and the improvements into one mortgage.

Purchase + Renovate

Purchase a home and finance repairs in a single loan—no separate construction financing needed.

Refinance + Improve

Refinance your current mortgage and fund improvements to increase comfort and value.

Renovate Before Move-In

Complete renovations before moving in so your home is exactly what you want from day one.

Build Equity Immediately

Increase your home's value from day one through strategic, financed improvements.

Instead of high-interest credit cards or personal loans, this provides a mortgage-based solution with competitive rates and long-term repayment options.

Why Work With a Renovation Loan Specialist?

Renovation loans are more complex than standard mortgages—requiring contractor coordination, project scopes, escrow management, and approval based on future value. Not every lender offers these programs, and many have limited experience.

Working with an experienced loan officer helps ensure:

Proper Loan Structuring

Your loan is set up correctly from the start, avoiding costly restructuring later.

Compliant Contractor Bids

Contractor bids meet program requirements so there are no surprises at review.

Timelines Stay on Track

Proactive coordination keeps your renovation and closing timeline moving.

Fewer Delays & Issues

Experience means anticipating and preventing common approval roadblocks.

Programs

Renovation Loan Options

Multiple programs are available depending on your situation, property type, and eligibility.

Streamlined Conventional Renovation Loans

HomeStyle® & CHOICERenovation®

  • Conventional financing with flexible guidelines
  • Primary, second homes, and some investment properties
  • Higher loan limits vs FHA
  • Allows cosmetic + structural + some luxury upgrades
  • Typically requires stronger credit

VA Renovation Loans

For Veterans & Service Members

  • For eligible veterans and service members
  • Little to no down payment
  • Competitive VA-backed rates
  • Covers necessary repairs
  • Requires experienced lender

USDA Renovation Loans

Rural-Eligible Areas

  • Rural-eligible areas across ND, MN, SD
  • No down payment
  • Purchase + repairs combined
  • Income limits apply
  • Ideal for smaller communities

FHA 203(k) Renovation Loans

Government-Backed Flexibility

  • 3.5% down payment
  • Flexible credit requirements
  • Streamline (light) + Standard (full rehab)
  • Structural + cosmetic improvements allowed
Example

Example Purchase Scenario

Home Purchase Price $220,000
Renovation Budget $45,000
Total Financed Amount* $265,000

Instead of buying the home and trying to fund repairs separately, a renovation loan can combine the purchase and approved improvement costs into one mortgage. That can make budgeting simpler and help buyers finance needed updates like roofing, flooring, kitchens, bathrooms, or structural repairs.

*Before down payment/adjustments. Actual loan structure, contingency reserves, appraisal, and eligibility vary by program and borrower qualifications.
