# Employee Directory & Profiles

Dashboard section: (none — reached via the "HR" menu's Team Directory, or your own profile via the header Settings gear icon)

## What it is
Every active employee has a profile — contact info, social links, an optional avatar, an optional business card image, up to two QR codes, an email signature, and (view-only for the employee) documents an admin uploaded to their file. The Team Directory lets anyone look up a coworker's Contact Card; the Settings panel lets you edit your own profile. A separate, richer "business card generator" (front/back HTML template with a photo, logo, and QR) is built and saved for employees inside Admin Settings, not in the self-service profile editor.

## How to find it
Open the "HR" menu in the top nav — the "Team Directory" list at the bottom shows every active employee by name; click one to open their Contact Card. To edit your own profile, click the gear icon ("Settings") in the header toolbar and use the "Profile" tab of the Settings panel.

## Common tasks

### Look up a coworker's contact info
HR menu → click a name in Team Directory. The Contact Card shows their avatar, name, role/team, phone, display email, website, online application link, NMLS #, social icons, any custom links, a downloadable business card image (if one is set), QR codes (if set), and their email signature with a "Copy for Outlook" button that copies it as rich HTML (with a plain-text fallback) so it pastes into an email with formatting intact.

### Edit your own contact info, social links, or signature
Header gear icon → Settings → Profile tab. You can change your photo, upload a business card image and up to two QR codes (with optional labels), and edit Contact Info (phone, display email, website, online app URL, NMLS #), Social Media (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube, X/Twitter), Business Social (Facebook Business, Google My Business, Nextdoor), and your Email Signature (pasted as raw HTML), then click "Save Profile". Custom links and your avatar's crop/reposition are admin-managed, not self-editable.

### View documents on file
Settings → Documents tab lists files an admin uploaded to your employee record (read-only) with a download link for each — you cannot upload your own.

### Generate a business card (admin/manager only)
Inside Admin Settings → Employees tab, opening an employee's profile and going to its "Business Card" sub-tab lets an admin or manager pick a brand (MSFG Home Loans / Compass Home Loans), a job title, whether to include a photo, and whether/which QR code to print on the back, then click "Generate" to build a front/back HTML template pre-filled from that employee's profile photo, phone, email, website, and NMLS #. "Save" persists the template; "PNG Front"/"PNG Back" export print-shop-ready 600 dpi image files; "Open & Print" opens a 3.5×2" two-page print layout. The photo is optional — unchecking "Include photo" generates a photo-free layout, and any admin-set avatar crop/reposition carries through to the card's headshot circle.

## FAQ
**Q: Why does my avatar look cropped differently on my business card than in my profile photo?**
A: An admin or manager can set a custom crop/reposition on your avatar from Admin Settings; both your Contact Card and any generated business card honor that same crop position.

**Q: I generated a business card PNG — why doesn't it show up as my Business Card image?**
A: The PNG buttons just download a print-ready file to your computer. To make an image appear as your downloadable "Business Card" on your Contact Card, it has to be uploaded separately through Settings → Profile → Business Card upload (or done for you by an admin).

**Q: Can I add my own custom links to my Contact Card?**
A: Not from your own Settings panel — custom links are added and managed by an admin or manager through Admin Settings.

**Q: Why is "Copy for Outlook" better than selecting and copying the signature text myself?**
A: It copies both the rendered HTML and a plain-text fallback in one clipboard write, so pasting into Outlook (or another rich-text target) keeps the signature's formatting instead of pasting as plain text.
